# Sales Battle Card Generator

This skill creates actionable battle cards for consultative B2B sales environments where customers often evaluate multiple solutions.

## Battle Card Structure

### 1. Company Overview

Provide concise competitor intel:

```
Company: [Name]
Founded: [Year]
Positioning: [How they describe themselves in one sentence]
Target customers: [Their ICP]
Delivery model: [e.g., managed service, self-serve platform, hybrid]
Key strengths: [What they're genuinely good at]
```

### 2. Approach to Market: Positioning

**Our positioning vs. Competitor:**

Create a comparison table with this structure:

| Criteria | Competitor | Us | So What |
|----------|------------|-----|---------|
| [Dimension 1] | [Their approach] | [Our approach] | ✅ **Strength:** [How we win] <br>⚠️ **Weakness:** [Where we're weaker] |
| [Dimension 2] | [Their approach] | [Our approach] | ✅ **Strength:** [How we win] <br>⚠️ **Weakness:** [Where we're weaker] |

**Choose dimensions that reflect how buyers actually make decisions in this market.** Adapt these to what matters for the specific product and competitive context. Some common starting points:

- How the product is delivered or consumed
- How pricing is structured
- How quickly customers see value
- The level of control or customization customers have
- What integrations or ecosystem the product fits into
- What kind of support or service model is offered
- Where the product is strongest vs. most limited

**For each "So What":**
- Be honest about strengths AND weaknesses
- Focus on customer outcomes, not features
- Use customer language, not internal jargon

### 4. When to Engage / Not Engage / Compete Strategically

**Engage (High win probability):**

*Use case signals:*
- [Specific use case where you excel]
- [Customer pain point you solve uniquely]

*Buyer language signals:*
- Customer says: "[specific phrase]"
- Customer asks about: "[specific capability]"

**Don't Engage (Low win probability):**

*Use case signals:*
- [Use case where competitor is stronger]
- [Customer need you can't meet]

*Buyer language signals:*
- Customer says: "[phrase indicating bad fit]"
- Customer prioritizes: "[capability you lack]"

**Compete Strategically (Mixed signals):**

*When to compete:*
- Long-term strategic account
- Competitive displacement opportunity
- Use case aligns with roadmap even if not ready today

*Decision factors:*
- Customer's flexibility on timeline
- Openness to phased approach
- Strategic value beyond immediate deal

### 5. Attack and Defense

**When Competitor Comes Up in Conversation:**

*Their strength / Your response:*
- **If they say:** "[Competitor strength]"
- **You say:** "That's true, they're strong at [acknowledge strength]. What we're hearing from customers is [pivot to your differentiation]. How important is [your strength] to your team?"

**Common Objections & Responses:**

*Objection 1: "[Common objection]"*

**Response:**
- Acknowledge: "[Validate their concern]"
- Reframe: "[Shift perspective]"
- Proof: "[Evidence/example]"
- Question: "[Confirm understanding or uncover real concern]"

*Objection 2: "[Common objection]"*

**Response:**
- [Same structure]

**When to Bring Up Competitor First:**

Do this when you know they're evaluating them and you want to control the narrative:

"A lot of teams evaluating us are also looking at [Competitor]. They're a solid choice for [their strength]. The teams that ultimately choose us tell us it's because [your key differentiator]. Is [differentiator] important to your use case?"

## Proof Points to Weave Throughout

Include 2-3 concrete win stories:

**Win Story Template:**
```
Customer: [Industry/size]
Situation: [What they were trying to solve]
Alternatives considered: [Including this competitor]
Why we won: [Specific reasons]
Outcome: [Quantifiable results]
Quotable: "[Customer soundbite if available]"
```

## Using This Battle Card

**Before a call:**
- Review discovery questions
- Note which engagement category likely applies

**During a call:**
- Listen for signals (use cases, language)
- Ask discovery questions naturally in conversation
- Position based on what matters to THIS customer

**After a call:**
- Update CRM with competitive intel learned
- Share new objections or positioning insights with PMM team

## Keeping It Fresh

Battle cards should be updated:
- Quarterly (minimum)
- After major competitive wins/losses
- When competitor changes strategy or positioning
- When you learn new intel from sales calls

**Share feedback with PMM team:**
- New objections you're hearing
- Discovery questions that work/don't work
- Positioning that resonates/falls flat
