# Customer-Facing One-Pager Generator

This skill creates concise, compelling one-pagers for sales and customer conversations.

## One-Pager Structure (Single Page, Scannable)

### Header Section

**Visual hierarchy:**
- Company logo (top left)
- One-pager title (clear, benefit-focused)
- Tagline (optional, 1 sentence max)

**Title Best Practices:**
- Lead with the audience or capability: "[Audience] for [Outcome]"
*(Examples: "Enterprise Security for Regulated Industries" or "Managed Onboarding for Fast-Growing Teams")*
- Avoid jargon; be specific about who/what

### 1. The Problem (2-3 sentences)

State the specific challenge this audience or capability addresses.

**Framework:**
```
[Target audience] needs [specific outcome].

Current approaches [describe status quo and its limitations].

This creates [business impact / cost / risk].
```

**Tips:**
- Use customer language, not internal terminology
- Be specific — avoid vague claims; quantify where possible *(e.g. "teams spend 40% of their time on X" beats "teams struggle with X")*
- Make it relatable to the reader

### 2. Our Approach (3-4 sentences)

Explain your solution clearly and concisely.

**Framework:**
```
[Your company] provides [what you deliver].

[Key differentiator 1] ensures [customer benefit].

[Key differentiator 2] enables [customer benefit].

This means [ultimate outcome for customer].
```

**Tips:**
- Focus on "what" and "how," not exhaustive "why"
- Connect features directly to benefits
- Keep it jargon-free

### 3. Key Differentiators (3-5 bullets)

What makes your approach unique and valuable?

**Format each as:**
```
**[Differentiator]:** [Brief explanation of customer impact]
```

**Adapt these to what genuinely sets your product apart.** Some common starting points:
*(Replace with differentiators that reflect your actual competitive advantage)*
- **[Quality or reliability signal]:** How you ensure consistent, trustworthy output
- **[Speed or scale advantage]:** How quickly customers can get value or grow
- **[Control or transparency]:** What visibility or customisation customers have
- **[Integration or ecosystem fit]:** How easily it slots into existing workflows
- **[Service or support model]:** What hands-on help customers get

**Tips:**
- Lead with the differentiator (bold), then explain impact
- Focus on outcomes, not just features
- Use metrics where possible

### 4. Proof Points (2-3 items)

Build credibility with evidence.

**Options:**
- Customer testimonial (name, company, role)
- Usage statistics *(e.g. "Used by [X] companies in [industry]")*
- Performance metrics *(e.g. "[X]% faster / cheaper / more accurate")*
- Case study reference
- Notable customer logos

**Format:**
```
"[Customer quote that validates key value prop]"
— [Name, Title, Company]
```

Or:

```
**[Metric]:** [Context about what it proves]
```

**Tips:**
- Choose proof points that validate your key differentiators
- Real customer names > anonymous statistics when possible
- Specific > vague ("73% faster" beats "significantly faster")

### 5. Call to Action

What should the reader do next?

**Options:**
- "Contact [name/team] to discuss your [use case] needs"
- "Visit [URL] to learn more"
- "Schedule a demo: [link]"
- "Request access: [email]"

**Tips:**
- One clear CTA, not multiple competing options
- Make it easy (include contact info, link, or next step)
- Match the sales motion (self-serve vs. high-touch)

## Design Considerations

**Layout:**
- Single page, readable without zooming
- White space between sections
- 2-column layout works well for longer text
- Use hierarchy (headers, bold, bullets) to guide eye

**Tone:**
- Professional but not stuffy
- Confident but not arrogant
- Specific but not overwhelming

**Length:**
- Problem: 2-3 sentences
- Approach: 3-4 sentences
- Differentiators: 3-5 bullets
- Proof: 2-3 items
- Total: ~200-300 words

## Common One-Pager Types

**Audience-Specific** *(e.g. a one-pager targeting a specific buyer persona or customer segment)*
- Focus: Who they are, what they care about, how you serve them specifically
- Differentiators: Why your product is the right fit for this audience
- Proof: Results from customers like them

**Capability-Specific** *(e.g. a one-pager for a specific product feature or service tier)*
- Focus: What you deliver, how it works, who it's for
- Differentiators: Service or product model advantages
- Proof: Delivery metrics, customer testimonials

**Use Case-Specific** *(e.g. a one-pager for a particular problem or workflow)*
- Focus: The use case challenge, your solution approach
- Differentiators: Why your approach works for this use case
- Proof: Use case success stories

## Usage Guidelines

**When to create a one-pager:**
- Entering a new audience segment
- Launching a new capability or service
- Sales keeps asking "do we have something on [X]?"
- Customer conversations need a leave-behind

**When NOT to create a one-pager:**
- Information exists elsewhere and can be easily found
- Audience is too broad (needs to be specific)
- Content would be outdated quickly (use web page instead)

## Testing Your One-Pager

Before finalizing, ask:
1. Can someone unfamiliar with your company understand the value in 60 seconds?
2. Is it clear WHO this is for and WHAT problem it solves?
3. Would a salesperson know when to use this?
4. Is there proof that validates the key claims?
5. Is the CTA clear and easy to act on?

If any answer is "no," revise that section.
