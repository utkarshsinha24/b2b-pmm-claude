# Product Launch Brief Generator

This skill creates internal launch briefs that align cross-functional teams on positioning, messaging, and GTM execution.

## Who This Brief Is For

**Primary audience:** Product marketing, product management, sales, customer success, marketing
**Purpose:** Align teams on launch narrative, target audience, and execution plan
**When to create:** 4-6 weeks before launch (earlier for major launches)

## Launch Brief Structure

### 1. Executive Summary

**One paragraph covering:**
- What we're launching
- Who it's for
- Why it matters
- When it's happening

**Template:**
```
On [date], we're launching [product/feature name], [one-sentence description]. This [product/feature] enables [target audience] to [key outcome/benefit]. This launch is important because [strategic rationale: revenue impact, competitive positioning, customer retention, market expansion]. The launch includes [key activities: announcement, sales enablement, customer comms, etc.].
```

### 2. Product/Feature Overview

**What we're launching:**
- Product/feature name
- Brief description (2-3 sentences)
- Key capabilities (3-5 bullets)
- What's new vs. what existed before

**What's in scope / out of scope:**
- Included in this launch
- Not included (coming later or intentionally excluded)
- Known limitations or caveats

### 3. Strategic Context

**Why we're launching this:**
- Market opportunity or gap
- Customer pain point we're solving
- Competitive landscape context
- How this fits into broader product strategy

**Success criteria:**
- Revenue impact (pipeline, bookings, expansion)
- Adoption metrics (MAU, feature usage, activation)
- Customer impact (NPS, retention, support tickets)
- Competitive wins (displacement, win rate vs. competitor)

### 4. Target Audience & Positioning

**Primary audience:**
```
Who: [Specific customer segment/persona]
Current situation: [What they do today / pain they experience]
Why they'll care: [Key benefit specific to them]
```

**Secondary audiences:** (if applicable)
- [Audience 2]: [Why they care]
- [Audience 3]: [Why they care]

**Positioning:**
```
For [target audience]
Who [specific need/pain point]
[Product/feature] is [category/description]
That [key differentiated value]
Unlike [alternatives]
We [unique capability]
```

**How this fits with overall positioning:**
- Does this reinforce existing positioning or introduce new elements?
- How does this connect to overarching brand narrative?
- What does this signal to the market about our direction?

## Launch Brief Best Practices

**Start early:**
- Create brief 4-6 weeks before launch
- Iterate with stakeholders
- Lock messaging 2 weeks before launch

**Keep it focused:**
- Resist scope creep
- Clear on what's in/out of scope
- Prioritize ruthlessly

**Make it actionable:**
- Assign owners to every deliverable
- Set clear deadlines
- Define success metrics upfront

**Align stakeholders:**
- Review with product, sales, CS, marketing
- Get explicit sign-off on positioning and messages
- Update brief as decisions get made

**Update as you learn:**
- Capture customer feedback post-launch
- Document what worked / didn't work
- Use insights for next launch

## Common Launch Types

**New product:** Requires full brief, all sections
**Major feature:** Focus on positioning fit and sales enablement
**Minor enhancement:** Lighter brief, focus on customer comms
**Beta/pilot:** Emphasize learning goals and feedback mechanisms
