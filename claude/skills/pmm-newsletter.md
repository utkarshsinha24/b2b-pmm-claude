# PMM Update Newsletter

This skill creates consistent, scannable newsletters that keep cross-functional stakeholders informed on product marketing activities.

## Newsletter Purpose

**Goals:**
- Maintain visibility into PMM work across the organization
- Share wins and customer insights
- Flag important updates that impact other teams
- Build credibility through consistent communication

**Audience:**
- Product, Sales, Customer Success, Marketing
- Leadership team (optional depending on distribution)

**Frequency:** 
- Weekly or bi-weekly (pick one and stick to it)
- Same day/time each cycle

## Newsletter Structure

### Subject Line Format

```
PMM Update: w/e [Date]
```

Or for bi-weekly:
```
PMM Update: [Date Range]
```

**Keep it consistent** - same format every time so people recognize it.

### Email Body

**Header:**
```
**PMM update: w/e [Date]**
*Latest news and updates from the product marketing team.*
```

---

### Section 1: Headline Win (The Hook)

**Purpose:** Lead with the most important or exciting update to grab attention

**Format:**
```
**🚀 [Headline]**
[2-3 sentences explaining what happened and why it matters]
[Link to relevant material] | [Link to Slack thread if applicable]
```

**Examples:**
- "🚀 **[Named customer] selects us over [key competitor]**"
- "🚀 **First $[X]K+ deal closed using new positioning**"
- "🚀 **[Product/feature] featured in [publication]**"

**Tips:**
- Make it specific and concrete
- Include impact (revenue, customer name, strategic value)
- Use emoji sparingly but effectively for visual break
- Keep it brief - save details for other sections if needed

---

### Section 2: Key Updates (Last [X] Weeks)

**Purpose:** Organized snapshot of major completed work

**Format:**
```
**🏁 Key updates in the last [X] weeks:**

**[Category 1: e.g., Competitive positioning]** → [What shipped]. [Brief impact or context]. [Link if available]

**[Category 2: e.g., Sales enablement]** → [What shipped]. [Brief impact or context]. [Link if available]

**[Category 3: e.g., Customer insights]** → [What learned]. [Brief impact or context]. [Link if available]
```

**Common categories:**
- Competitive positioning
- Sales enablement
- Product launches
- Customer insights
- Content/collateral
- Case studies/proof points
- Messaging updates
- Market insights

**Tips per update:**
- Lead with the category (bold)
- Use → to separate category from update (visual clarity)
- Keep each to 1-2 sentences
- Include links where relevant
- Focus on "done" items, not "in progress"

**Example:**
```
**Competitive positioning** → Published new battle card and positioning guide for [key competitor]. Sales team now have a Q&A guide for competitive conversations. [Next up: battle card for [next competitor]]

**Sales enablement** → Complete enablement suite now live: [segment A] one-pager, [segment B] one-pager, and updated pitch deck. Sales can now confidently pitch our full offering.

**Customer insights** → [Customer name] case study recorded with their [exec title] joining the conversation. Having executives involved created a stronger narrative about [key value theme] than customer-only interviews.
```

---

### Section 3: Looking Ahead

**Purpose:** Preview upcoming work to set expectations and invite input

**Format:**
```
**📅 Looking ahead**
* [Upcoming initiative 1]
* [Upcoming initiative 2]
* [Upcoming initiative 3]
* [Open question or request for input]
```

**Tips:**
- Keep it to 3-5 bullets
- Mix immediate priorities (this week) with near-term (next 2-4 weeks)
- Include one question or collaboration opportunity if relevant
- Be realistic - only include what will actually happen

**Example:**
```
**📅 Looking ahead**
* Expanding partner newsletter format to all CS reps (July) - if it drives similar engagement, could transform customer comms
* July What's New edition going out to customers
* [Product area] webpage refresh
* Q3 product launch planning kicks off next week
```

---

### Section 4: What We're Reading/Learning (Optional)

**Purpose:** Share interesting insights or resources

**Format:**
```
**📖 What we're reading**
* [Article/resource title with link]: [One sentence on why it's interesting]
```

**Tips:**
- Keep it to 1-2 items
- Choose content relevant to broader team (not just PMM-specific)
- Explain why it matters in one sentence
- This section is optional - skip if nothing compelling to share

**Example:**
```
**📖 What we're reading**
* [Lenny's Newsletter on AI product positioning](link): Great framework for talking about AI capabilities without overpromising - relevant for our messaging work
```

## Newsletter Best Practices

### Writing Style

**Do:**
- Write like you talk (conversational but professional)
- Be specific (names, numbers, outcomes)
- Use active voice
- Keep it scannable (people skim)
- Link to additional details rather than overwhelming with info

**Don't:**
- Bury the lede (lead with what matters)
- Use jargon or acronyms without context
- Write long paragraphs (break it up)
- Include work that's not relevant to audience
- Apologize for length or updates

### Content Selection

**Include:**
- ✅ Completed work with external impact
- ✅ Big wins worth celebrating
- ✅ Insights that inform other teams' work
- ✅ Updates that change how teams should operate

**Exclude:**
- ❌ Internal process updates (unless they affect others)
- ❌ Work-in-progress without clear milestone
- ❌ Information already widely known
- ❌ Overly tactical details that only matter to PMM

### Visual Formatting

**Use these elements:**
- **Bold** for categories and emphasis
- → for separating category from update
- Links to provide details without cluttering
- Emoji (sparingly) for visual section breaks
- Bullets for easy scanning
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)

**Avoid:**
- Long paragraphs
- Walls of text
- Too many formatting styles
- Excessive emoji
- Complex tables or layouts

### Distribution & Timing

**When to send:**
- End of week (Thursday/Friday) to recap
- Same day/time each cycle for consistency
- Avoid Monday mornings (gets buried)

**Where to send:**
- Email to stakeholder list
- Optional: Post summary in Slack channel
- Archive in shared drive or wiki

**Who to include:**
- Core: Product, Sales, CS, Marketing leads
- Extended: Anyone who asks or regularly engages
- Leadership: Opt-in rather than forcing on them

## Adapting the Newsletter

**For smaller teams:** 
- Reduce frequency (bi-weekly vs weekly)
- Combine categories
- Shorter "Looking ahead" section

**For larger organizations:**
- Add metrics section (pipeline impact, content engagement)
- Include team member spotlights
- Add "Requests/needs from team" section

**For leadership audience:**
- Lead with business impact
- Include strategic context
- Add competitive intelligence section
- Quantify outcomes more

## Measuring Effectiveness

**Good signs:**
- People respond with questions or input
- Stakeholders reference newsletter in meetings
- Teams proactively ask when it's late
- Other teams ask to be added to distribution

**Red flags:**
- No engagement (no replies, questions, mentions)
- Stakeholders still ask about things you covered
- You struggle to find content each cycle
- It feels like busy work

If you see red flags, either adjust format/content or reconsider if newsletter is the right vehicle.
