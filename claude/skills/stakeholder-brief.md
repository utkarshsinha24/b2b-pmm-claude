# Internal Stakeholder Brief

This skill creates concise internal briefs that give teams context and guidance on new materials, positioning, or initiatives.

## Brief Purpose

**When to create a stakeholder brief:**
- Launching new sales collateral (decks, one-pagers, battle cards)
- Rolling out new positioning or messaging
- Introducing new processes or frameworks
- Sharing customer insights that inform strategy
- Providing guidance on strategic initiatives

**Goals:**
- Ensure teams understand WHY this exists
- Clarify WHEN and HOW to use it
- Prevent misuse or confusion
- Drive adoption and proper implementation

### 2. Purpose & Context

**What this is:**
[1-2 sentences describing the material/initiative]

**Why we created it:**
[2-3 sentences on strategic context]
- What problem does this solve?
- What gap does this fill?
- Why now?

**Example:**
```
What this is:
A product roadmap presentation deck for H1 2024, designed for QBRs and strategic account meetings.

Why we created it:
As we pivot toward enterprise-first GTM, we need to elevate conversations beyond tactical updates to strategic partnership discussions. This deck initiates dialogue about our roadmap, gathers early customer feedback, and positions us as forward-thinking partners. It supports our goal to increase enterprise deal velocity and average contract value.
```

### 3. How to Use This

**Primary use cases:**
1. **[Use case 1]:** [When and how]
2. **[Use case 2]:** [When and how]
3. **[Use case 3]:** [When and how]

**Step-by-step guidance:**
```
When: [Triggering situation]
Who: [Which team member / role]
How: [Process to follow]
Expected outcome: [What success looks like]
```

**Example:**
```
Primary use cases:
1. **QBRs with strategic accounts:** Present roadmap in context of customer's strategic priorities to gather feedback and build partnership momentum
2. **Executive briefings for new enterprise prospects:** Demonstrate product vision and maturity during evaluation process
3. **Renewal conversations with at-risk accounts:** Reinforce commitment to innovation and address future-state concerns

Step-by-step guidance:
When: Scheduling a QBR or strategic check-in with an enterprise account
Who: AE or CSM leading the account relationship
How:
1. Review customer's stated priorities and pain points in CRM
2. Customize 2-3 roadmap slides most relevant to their needs
3. Present as "preview and feedback session" not "sales pitch"
4. Document feedback and questions in CRM
5. Follow up on specific interest areas within 48 hours
Expected outcome: Deeper strategic conversation, roadmap feedback, and strengthened partnership perception
```

## Brief Formats for Different Situations

### Sales Collateral Brief
Focus on: Use cases, talking points, when NOT to use, competitive context

### Process/Framework Brief  
Focus on: Step-by-step guidance, examples, edge cases, support resources

### Positioning Update Brief
Focus on: What changed and why, migration plan, training needs, FAQs

### Customer Insights Brief
Focus on: Key findings, implications for teams, recommended actions, supporting data

## Writing Best Practices

**Keep it to one page:**
- Dense information is fine, long rambling is not
- Use bullets and short paragraphs
- Make it scannable
- Link to details rather than including everything

**Be specific:**
- Concrete examples > abstract guidance
- Actual scenarios > theoretical frameworks  
- Names and numbers > vague statements

**Make it actionable:**
- Clear next steps
- Explicit dos and don'ts
- Contact information for questions
- Links to all referenced materials

**Test before distributing:**
- Share with 2-3 team members first
- Ask: "Is this clear?" "Would you know when to use this?"
- Revise based on feedback
- Consider running through an example scenario together

## Distribution Strategy

**Who needs this brief:**
- Everyone who will use the material/initiative (obviously)
- Their managers (for context and reinforcement)
- Adjacent teams who might be affected
- Anyone who asks "why did we create this?"

**How to distribute:**
- Email to stakeholder list with brief attached
- Post in relevant Slack channels
- Present in team meetings for major initiatives
- Add to wiki/knowledge base for future reference

**Follow-up:**
- Schedule office hours for questions
- Check in with early adopters for feedback
- Update brief based on learnings
- Celebrate successes and wins in team updates

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Too much information:**
- Brief becomes a lengthy document nobody reads
- Solution: Link to details, keep brief focused on "how to use"

**Unclear audience:**
- Everyone and no one is the target
- Solution: Specify exactly who this is for in header

**No examples:**
- Abstract guidance is hard to operationalize
- Solution: Include at least one concrete scenario

**Missing the "why":**
- Teams don't understand strategic context
- Solution: Lead with purpose before diving into details

**No feedback mechanism:**
- You never learn what's working or not
- Solution: Make it easy to provide input and commit to iterating
